--- FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQs) ---

Q: What is the cost of dental braces?
A: The cost of dental braces at the clinic is Ksh 75,000 per jaw and Ksh 150,000 for both jaws. A flexible payment plan is available — patients can pay 50% upfront and the remaining 50% over the course of treatment.

Q: What is the cost of dental implants?
A: Dental implants cost Ksh 100,000 for the implant and Ksh 50,000 for the crown. If a bone graft is required, there is an additional cost of Ksh 50,000 – Ksh 100,000 per tooth. A 50/50 flexible payment plan is available.

Q: What is the cost of root canal treatment?
A: Root canal treatment costs between Ksh 20,000 and Ksh 22,000 per tooth.

Q: What is the cost of teeth cleaning?
A: A teeth cleaning session costs Ksh 7,500.

Q: What is the cost of dental cosmetics?
A: Cosmetic dental treatments (bridges, crowns, veneers) start at Ksh 7,500 for masking, and go up to Ksh 40,000 for a crown and Ksh 45,000 – Ksh 55,000 for veneers, per tooth. Costs may be negotiable for multiple teeth.

Q: What is the cost of paediatric (children's) dental treatment?
A: Paediatric services include teeth extraction and fillings. Baby tooth extraction costs Ksh 3,500 per tooth; tooth filling costs Ksh 4,000 per tooth.

Q: What is the cost of dental X-rays?
A: Dental X-rays cost between Ksh 1,000 and Ksh 2,000 depending on the type recommended by the dentist.

Q: What is the cost of teeth whitening?
A: Teeth whitening costs Ksh 21,500 – Ksh 30,000 for all teeth that require whitening, depending on the whitening method used.

Q: What is the cost of emergency dental pain management?
A: Emergency consultations are charged at Ksh 3,000. No extra charges are applied for last-minute or emergency appointments.

Q: What is the cost of tooth extraction?
A: Baby tooth extraction: Ksh 3,500 per tooth. Regular tooth extraction: Ksh 5,000 per tooth. Surgical extractions: Ksh 10,000 – Ksh 15,000.

Q: What is the cost of dental fillings?
A: Dental fillings are charged at Ksh 7,500 per tooth.

Q: What are the accepted modes of payment?
A: The clinic accepts cash, M-Pesa (mobile money), and insurance.

Q: Do you accept insurance?
A: Yes. The clinic has partnered with several insurance providers in Kenya to offer accessible and affordable care.

Q: Where is the clinic located?
A: Park Suite Building, 6th Floor, Parklands, Nairobi. You can also book an appointment online via the clinic's website or social media pages.

--- PAYMENT PLANS ---
- Flexible 50/50 payment plan available for high-cost procedures such as braces and dental implants.
- Payment can be split: 50% at the start of treatment, and 50% paid over the course of the treatment.
